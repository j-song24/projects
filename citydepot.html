City Depot

Team: Khushi Shah, Sasha Meng, Jeffrey Chou, Jesse Song

Summary and Transformational Goal

City Depot is an indiviudal, four player game with the goal of producing meaningful data that will aid self-reflection and self-knowledge processes relevant to the workplace.

Background

Employers need actionable, reliable information that they can trust, that guides them away from undesired bias, and that will help them to understand their employees better. Our clients at GroupProject tasked us with creating a game that would help players reflect on their behavior (their own or fellow players') effectively and in a way that does not reinforce societal biases.

Theory

Collectivism vs. Individualism

  • - Collectivism - Group-oriented, altruistic, and empathetic
  • - Individualism - Oriented towards the self and thinking on behalf of oneself

Through the game mechanics, we force players to make decisions that either benefit themselves or benefit other players. Not only does this indicate to the player whether or not theyâ€™re collectivist or individualist, but it allows them to reflect on their decision. Even if a more collectivist player makes an individualist action, we hope during gameplay, the player experiences cognitive dissonance and reflects on their decision. They may ask questions to themselves like: Why am I playing so selfishly when normally I care about team dynamics? We hope that throughout the course of the game, our players think about their personal traits and have fun at the same time.
