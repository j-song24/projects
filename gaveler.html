Gaveler

An application that gives users a voice.

Background

Many people know about the Brock Turner case, but how many people know who the acting judge was? Who was the person responsible for the ruling? Let's say you have a hearing with a judge at your local county court for a traffic violation. Would you want to see other people's experiences with this judge? Whether or not they were fair and respectful?

At Dog and Rooster, I worked on redesigning a current in-house app called Gaveler. The idea behind the app was to help users share their experiences about judges and cases. Currently, there isnâ€™t a way for people to effectively express their opinions. Sure, there are applications like Yelp, that allow users to rate restaurants and establishments, but judges are rarely under scrutiny and critique by the general public.

Why Redesign?

  • Outdated UI
  • Doesn't emphasize interaction between users enough
  • Posting judges/cases with current design is tedious and uninteresting
  • Little user retention

    • My Solutions

    Redesign Goals

    I focused on three main goals/actions I wanted the users to do:

    1. View/engage
    2. React
    3. Post

    From the competitive analysis, I created a list of improvements I wanted to make and categorized them based on which action/goal it would help achieve.

  • Create a more pleasurable posting experience (1) - Currently a lot of steps users must take
  • More categorization by hottest, trending, controversial, in the news, etc. to engage interest and help retain user base (1)
  • Entirely new section for shared experiences (more like a feed) (2,3)
  • Shareable collections (rather than a private watchlist) (1,3)
  • Onboarding (1) - Username, photo, add to collection, political affiliation, age
  • Q&A section where users can ask other users questions about specific judges/cases (1) - A way to give advice, elaborate on a shared experience, develop repertoire between users, just chat
